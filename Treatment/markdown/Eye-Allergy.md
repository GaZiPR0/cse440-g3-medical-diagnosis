# 👁️ Eye Allergies

> **In short:** Also called allergic conjunctivitis — red, itchy, watery eyes caused by the immune system overreacting to a harmless substance such as pollen, dust, or pet dander.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Also known as** | Allergic conjunctivitis |
| **Contagious?** | No |
| **Key sign** | Itchy, watery eyes |
| **Triggered by** | Allergens |

---

## Overview

**Eye allergies**, also called **allergic conjunctivitis**, happen for the same reason as other allergic reactions: the immune system, which is meant to protect the body, overreacts to something harmless. It treats substances like pollen, dust mites, mould, or pet dander as if they were dangerous and releases **histamine**, a chemical that causes swelling and inflammation. As a result, the blood vessels in the eyes swell, and the eyes turn red, watery, and itchy.

## Common Triggers & Types

- **Seasonal** — pollen from grass, weeds, and trees; the most common trigger, causing seasonal allergic conjunctivitis.
- **Perennial (year-round)** — indoor allergens like dust and pet dander cause chronic, year-round symptoms.
- **Contact** — cosmetics, perfume, and chemical products can lead to contact conjunctivitis.
- **Giant papillary** — a reaction to contact lenses that forms small bumps inside the eyelid, making the eyes red and sensitive whether or not lenses are being worn.

## Symptoms

- Itchy eyes
- Redness
- Watering
- Burning
- Swollen eyelids
- Usually affects both eyes

## Treatment

Many of the medications used for nasal allergies also help eye allergies. Over-the-counter eye drops and pills can offer fast relief.

**Other ways to reduce symptoms**

- Wear sunglasses outdoors to keep pollen and other allergens out of your eyes.
- Flush your eyes with preservative-free saline, or hold a cool, damp cloth over them.
- Use artificial tears to keep eyes moist and rinse away allergens.
- Remove your contact lenses.
- Try not to rub your eyes — rubbing only makes the irritation worse.

> ⚠️ **Contact a doctor without delay** if you have intense eye pain or any loss of vision.

## Prevention

- Keep windows closed during high-pollen seasons and use air conditioning.
- Reduce indoor allergens — wash bedding often and control dust and pet dander.
- Avoid known triggers wherever possible.
- Don't rub your eyes, and wash your hands after contact with animals.
