# ❤️ Coronary Arteriosclerosis

> **In short:** A hardening and narrowing of the arteries that supply the heart, caused by a buildup of fatty plaque. It reduces blood flow to the heart muscle and can cause chest pain or a heart attack.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Cardiovascular disease |
| **Affects** | Coronary arteries |
| **Main symptom** | Chest pain (angina) |
| **Largely** | Preventable |

---

## Overview

**Coronary arteriosclerosis** (also called coronary artery disease or atherosclerosis of the heart) happens when the arteries that carry blood to the heart become stiff and narrow. Over time, fatty deposits called **plaque** — made of cholesterol, fat, and other substances — build up along the artery walls. As the passage narrows, less oxygen-rich blood reaches the heart muscle, which can cause chest pain and, if an artery becomes blocked, a heart attack.

## Symptoms

- Chest pain or a tight feeling (angina)
- Shortness of breath
- Feeling tired a lot
- Dizziness
- Pain spreading to the arm, neck, or jaw

Some people have no symptoms until the disease is advanced. Chest discomfort is often triggered by physical activity or stress and eases with rest.

> ⚠️ **Heart-attack warning signs — call emergency services:** crushing chest pain or pressure, pain spreading to the arm, jaw, or back, cold sweat, nausea, and severe shortness of breath. Act immediately.

## Causes & Risk Factors

- High cholesterol and high blood pressure
- Smoking and heavy alcohol use
- Diabetes or high blood sugar
- Being overweight and physically inactive
- A diet high in salt and saturated fat
- Family history of heart disease and increasing age

## Diagnosis

- **ECG (electrocardiogram)** — records the heart's electrical activity.
- **Stress test** — checks how the heart performs during exercise.
- **Blood tests** — measure cholesterol and other markers.
- **Angiography / CT scan** — imaging that reveals narrowed or blocked arteries.

## Treatment

- **Eat healthier** — more fruit, vegetables, and whole grains; less salt and saturated fat.
- **Exercise safely** — regular activity as advised by your doctor.
- **Quit smoking** and cut down on alcohol.
- **Take prescribed medicines** for blood pressure, cholesterol, or blood sugar.

In more serious cases, procedures such as **angioplasty and stenting** (to widen an artery) or **bypass surgery** may be needed to restore blood flow.

## Prevention

- Keep a healthy weight.
- Stay physically active every day.
- Eat less salt and fatty food.
- Keep an eye on your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar.
- Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol.
