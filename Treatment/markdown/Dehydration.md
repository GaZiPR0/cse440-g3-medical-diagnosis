# 💧 Dehydration

> **In short:** Dehydration happens when your body loses more fluid than it takes in, leaving it without enough water to work properly. It can range from mild thirst to a medical emergency.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Fluid & electrolyte imbalance |
| **Most at risk** | Children and older adults |
| **Key sign** | Thirst and dark urine |
| **Prevention** | Regular fluids |

---

## Overview

**Dehydration** occurs when your body loses more water than it takes in, so it doesn't have enough fluid to carry out its normal functions. It can be caused by not drinking enough, or by losing too much fluid through sweating, fever, vomiting, or diarrhoea. Even mild dehydration can leave you feeling tired and unwell, while severe dehydration is dangerous and needs urgent medical care.

## Symptoms

**Mild to moderate:** thirst, dry mouth, tiredness, dizziness, headache, dark yellow urine, and passing less urine than usual.

**Severe (get help):** extreme thirst, very dark urine or none at all, rapid heartbeat, sunken eyes, confusion, and fainting.

> ⚠️ **See a doctor urgently** if you can't keep water down, have bloody or black stools, feel very confused or sleepy, or a child or older person shows signs of severe dehydration.

## Common Causes

- Not drinking enough, especially in hot weather
- Heavy sweating during exercise or heat
- Fever
- Vomiting and diarrhoea
- Frequent urination (for example with uncontrolled diabetes)

## What to Do About It

- Drink water often — small sips at a time.
- If you feel weak, use an oral rehydration solution (water with a little salt and sugar) to replace lost salts.
- Rest somewhere cool and out of the sun.
- See a doctor if you can't keep fluids down or feel very confused — severe cases may need fluids through a drip (IV).

## Prevention

- Drink enough water when it's hot or when you exercise.
- Eat fruits and vegetables with a high water content.
- Don't rely on caffeine or sugary drinks to hydrate.
- Check on children and older people, who dehydrate more easily.
- Drink extra fluids when you have a fever, vomiting, or diarrhoea.
