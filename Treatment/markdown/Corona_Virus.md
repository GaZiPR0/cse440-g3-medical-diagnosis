# 🦠 COVID-19 (Coronavirus)

> **In short:** COVID-19 is a contagious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It spreads mainly through droplets in the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes.

## Overview

**COVID-19** is a disease caused by a coronavirus called SARS-CoV-2. It spreads from person to person, mostly through small respiratory droplets and particles released when a sick person coughs, sneezes, speaks, or even just breathes near others. Most people develop mild to moderate illness and recover without special treatment, but some — especially older adults and those with existing health conditions — can become seriously ill.

## Symptoms

Symptoms usually appear 2–14 days after exposure and range from very mild to severe. Common ones include:

- Fever or chills
- Cough
- Feeling very tired
- Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
- Loss of smell or taste
- Sore throat, body aches, or headache

> ⚠️ **Emergency warning signs — seek help immediately:** trouble breathing, persistent chest pain or pressure, confusion, inability to stay awake, or bluish lips or face. Call emergency services right away.

## Diagnosis

- **PCR test** — a swab from the nose or throat is analysed in a lab; the most accurate method for detecting active infection.
- **Rapid antigen test** — gives results in minutes and can be done at home, though it is slightly less sensitive than PCR.

## Treatment & Care

Most cases can be managed at home with rest and self-care:

- Stay home and rest to let your body recover.
- Drink plenty of fluids to stay hydrated.
- Take paracetamol (acetaminophen) for fever or body pain.
- Isolate from others to avoid spreading the virus.

For people at higher risk, a doctor may prescribe antiviral medication. Severe cases may need hospital care with oxygen therapy or other support. See a doctor if breathing becomes difficult, symptoms get worse, or you feel very unwell.

## Prevention

- Stay up to date with recommended COVID-19 vaccines.
- Wash your hands often with soap, or use hand sanitiser.
- Cover your mouth and nose when you cough or sneeze.
- Wear a mask if you're sick or around people who are ill.
- Keep distance from people who are coughing or unwell, and ventilate indoor spaces.
